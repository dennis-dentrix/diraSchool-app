### Subsections:

#### **H3: Competency Tracking & Assessment**
"The heart of CBC management is tracking how students are developing across the eight competencies. A purpose-built system should:
- Allow teachers to log formative assessments tied to each competency
- Auto-aggregate competency progress across subjects and classes
- Generate reports showing which students are falling behind in specific competencies (e.g., 'Critical Thinking' below grade-level benchmarks)
- Help teachers plan intervention lessons based on competency gaps

DiraSchool's competency tracker lets a Grade 5 teacher input an observation—say, a group discussion exercise—and tag which competencies were demonstrated. The system then compares that student's progress to expected milestones. If a student hasn't shown growth in Learner Agency by midterm, the system flags it. Teachers spend 3–4 hours per term on assessment logging, not 15."

**Data point:**
"Kenyan schools implementing competency-based tracking improve identified intervention rates by 60% (Source: pilot data from 12 schools, 2024)."

#### **H3: Student Information & Admissions**
"From enrollment to reports, the system must handle Kenya-specific requirements:
- Digital intake forms (parent contact, guardian info, medical history)
- Fee structure tracking (with Kenya's complex per-term breaks and optional programs: extra tuition, lunch fees, development contributions, exam fees)
- Automated parent communication (SMS reminders in Swahili and English)
- Sibling management and family accounts
- Integration with national ID verification where applicable

Example: A parent enrolls a child for Class 3. The system auto-creates a fee profile based on your school's structure, sends them a welcome SMS, and syncs their details to the teacher's gradebook."

"[See how DiraSchool's admissions module works →](#student-management)" or "[Compare free vs. premium fee management features →](#pricing)"

#### **H3: Timetabling & Scheduling**
"CBC schools often use more flexible learning structures than traditional schools: project-based learning, small-group rotation, interdisciplinary units. A modern system should:
- Support custom lesson blocks (not just rigid 40-minute periods)
- Handle resource scheduling (labs, grounds, iPads)
- Let teachers publish their schedule to parents
- Alert when conflicts occur (teacher in two places, overcrowded resource)

Most schools spend 5–10 hours at the start of term building the timetable in Excel. With a visual timetable builder, this takes 2–3 hours. Changes mid-term (teacher illness, guest speaker) update in minutes, not hours of manual adjustment."

#### **H3: Gradebook & Academic Tracking**
"Even in CBC, some traditional academic data still matters: exam results, reading levels, math fluency. The system should:
- Support subject-level grades AND competency ratings
- Auto-calculate term summaries and class statistics
- Let teachers enter grades via web, mobile app, or bulk upload (for offline schools)
- Generate mid-term and final reports in seconds, not hours of manual formatting

A typical Grade 4 teacher has 45 students. Traditional gradebook entry: 90 minutes. DiraSchool: 20 minutes with smart templates."

**Data point:**
"Teacher time saved per term: 12–18 hours (equivalent to 2–3 full school days) when shifting from manual Excel-based tracking."

#### **H3: Fee Management & Finance**
"School fees in Kenya are complex: base fees, lunch, transport, uniforms, development funds, exam fees, activity fees. Some are per-term, some per-year, some optional. Parents miss payments, balances roll over.

A modern system should:
- Auto-calculate each student's fee bill from your school's structure
- Track which payments have been received and which are overdue
- Generate default notices (email + SMS) at 2 weeks, 4 weeks overdue
- Integrate with M-Pesa, bank transfers, and cash payment logs
- Run 'fee analytics'—show principal who owes what, by month, by class

Schools typically lose 3–5% of fee revenue due to tracking errors. A clean fee system recovers that. For a 400-student school at KES 50,000 average annual fee, that's KES 600,000–1,000,000 recovered per year."

- Push progress notifications to parents (SMS + app, in English & Swahili)

## SECTION 4: CBC SCHOOL MANAGEMENT SYSTEM CHECKLIST — WHAT TO LOOK FOR
**H2:** "Choosing the Right School Management System: A Checklist for Kenyan Schools"
**Word count:** 400–450

### Content Strategy:
- Provide a **practical checklist** (easy to scan, helps them evaluate competitors and see why DiraSchool wins)
- Use subheadings to group by priority

### Checklist Format:

**Critical Features (Must-Have):**
- ☑ Competency-based assessment (not just grades)
- ☑ Parent & teacher portals (mobile-friendly)
- ☑ Fee management with Kenya payment methods (M-Pesa, EFT, cash logs)
- ☑ SMS communication (English + Swahili)
- ☑ Offline capability (internet in rural Kenya is unreliable)
- ☑ Automatic report generation (saves hours per term)
- ☑ Student admissions module (no re-entry per term)

**Important Features (Should-Have):**
- ☑ Attendance tracking with absence notifications to parents
- ☑ Timetable builder (visual, handles resource conflicts)
- ☑ Exam/assessment scheduling
- ☑ Staff management (contracts, qualifications, leave)
- ☑ Bulk operations (import student lists, generate 50 reports at once)
- ☑ Data export (download everything, not locked in)
- ☑ API/integration with other tools you use

**Nice-to-Have Features:**
- ☑ Mobile app (vs. web only)
- ☑ Multi-language interface (Swahili, English, others)
- ☑ Student behavior/discipline tracking
- ☑ Library management
- ☑ Inventory management (for school supplies)
- ☑ Job board (for recruiting, parent notices)

**Implementation & Support (Equally Important):**
- ☑ Setup assistance during rollout (not just a manual PDF)
- ☑ Training for teachers, parents, office staff
- ☑ Local support team (can call during school day, not just email)
- ☑ Affordable pricing with no surprise fees
- ☑ Data security (encryption, backup, compliance with Kenya data laws)

**Red Flags to Avoid:**
- ❌ "Reports take 2+ hours to generate" (should be instant)
- ❌ No offline option (schools lose access if internet is down)
- ❌ Requires Excel export + manual reformatting (wastes the time savings)
- ❌ SMS costs charged per message with no transparency
- ❌ No local language support (staff and parents won't use it)
- ❌ Vendor can't explain how they keep data secure
- ❌ Pricing is per-teacher or per-student (adds up; should be flat or per-school)

"A system checking all three categories will feel different within weeks. Staff stop asking 'Where's the attendance sheet?' Admin happens with confidence. Parents stop complaining about lack of communication. The principal can focus on instruction instead of logistics."

## SECTION 5: CBC SCHOOL MANAGEMENT SYSTEM FAQ
**H2:** "Common Questions About School Management Systems in Kenya"

**Format:** Q&A, conversational tone. Each answer 80–120 words.

### FAQ 1: "We have a small school (150 students). Is a school management system worth it?"
"Absolutely. Smaller schools actually benefit more because admin time feels more painful—a principal or office manager juggling everything personally. Even at 150 students:
- Fee tracking saves 4–6 hours/month
- Report generation saves 8 hours per term
- Parent communication becomes possible (no time to call 50 parents individually)

Cost is usually KES 3,000–5,000/month for a small school. That's offset in 2–3 months by time recovered. A system pays for itself."

---

### FAQ 2: "We don't have great internet. Can we use a school management system offline?"
"Yes—this is non-negotiable in Kenya. A good system (like DiraSchool) has an offline mode:
- Teachers enter grades, attendance, notes without internet
- Data syncs automatically when connection returns
- No lost work, no 'your browser session expired' frustration

If a vendor says 'It requires internet always,' move on. Kenyan schools can't rely on stable connection."

---

### FAQ 3: "How do we migrate our student data from paper/Excel?"
"A quality vendor provides data migration as part of onboarding:
1. You provide a spreadsheet of current students (we have a template)
2. We clean & format it
3. We upload it into the system
4. You verify it's correct
5. Done—no manual re-entry

This usually takes 2–3 weeks for a school of 400 students. Avoid vendors who ask you to re-type everything."

---

### FAQ 4: "What if the school decides to switch systems later? Will we lose our data?"
"You shouldn't lose data if you choose wisely. Ask vendors:
- 'Can I export all my data to a standard format (CSV, Excel)?'
- 'Do you provide a data export API?'
- 'If we leave, what's the data handoff process?'

A reputable vendor will say 'Yes' to all three. Your data belongs to you. Run away from vendors who refuse."

---

### FAQ 5: "How much should a school management system cost?"
"Pricing varies by school size and feature set:
- **Tier 1** (small school, 150–300 students): KES 3,000–8,000/month
- **Tier 2** (medium school, 300–700 students): KES 8,000–15,000/month
- **Tier 3** (large school, 700+ students): KES 15,000–25,000/month

Some systems offer annual discounts (pay 10 months, get 2 free). Don't assume cheapest = best. A KES 2,000/month system that nobody uses costs more than a KES 10,000 system that saves 20 hours/week.

Ask about what's included: Is support included? SMS costs? Mobile app? Don't sign a contract that has surprise fees per SMS or per report."

## NOTES FOR CONTENT WRITER

1. **Use Kenya-specific examples liberally.** Readers in Nairobi, Kisumu, Mombasa should recognize their school experience. Generic examples feel tone-deaf.

2. **Data builds trust.** Where possible, cite actual studies or your own pilot data. "35% improvement" beats "significant improvement."

3. **Be honest about trade-offs.** If your system isn't perfect for very small schools (under 100), say so. Honesty builds credibility.

4. **Address the objection silently.** Readers think: "This sounds good, but our school is different" or "Why should we trust DiraSchool vs. competitors?" Pre-answer these in the copy.

5. **Avoid techie language.** Explain features in terms of *what the principal/teacher/parent experiences*, not how it works under the hood.

6. **The FAQ is your credibility section.** Make sure answers are direct, helpful, and not salesy. This is where trust is won or lost.
