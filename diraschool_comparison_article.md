## Why This Decision Matters for CBC Schools

Most school management systems were built for traditional 8-4-4 or international curricula. They weren't designed around Kenya's Competency-Based Curriculum — they added CBC as an afterthought.

The result: report cards that don't match the 4-level rubric (EE/ME/AE/BE), assessment tools built for percentages rather than competencies, and parent portals that show "Math: 72%" instead of competency progress summaries.

For a Kenyan primary school implementing CBC today, the curriculum alignment of your software is not a nice-to-have. It's the foundation.

Beyond CBC, Kenyan schools have specific needs that generic systems miss: M-Pesa payment reconciliation, SMS communication (not just email), term-aligned billing, Swahili language support, and operation in environments where internet connectivity is inconsistent.

The right system handles all of this natively. The wrong system creates a second layer of manual work to bridge the gaps.

## EBingwa: The Free-Forever Option With Real Traction

**Best for:** Schools with tight budgets, smaller enrollments, or those exploring digital management for the first time.

EBingwa's headline is hard to argue with: core features are free forever. No trial period. No hidden charges. The platform covers the essentials — CBC report cards, M-Pesa fee collection, digital attendance, parent communication, and student records — and has built an impressive base of 2,000+ active schools across Kenya.

**Where EBingwa is strongest:**

The free model removes the barrier that stops most Kenyan schools from going digital at all. A school with 120 students and a principal who does most of the admin themselves can get started without a budget conversation.

The mobile app (available on Google Play) means teachers can mark attendance and enter marks from their phones — useful in schools where staff don't have dedicated desktop computers.

The 2,000+ school base also means the platform has been stress-tested against real Kenyan school workflows. Common pain points have likely been addressed through feedback.

Before committing your school's data to any platform, it's worth asking about their long-term sustainability.

Feature depth is another consideration. The platform covers the essentials well, but schools with complex needs — multi-campus operations, detailed fee structures with many line items, or granular audit trails — may find the feature set limits them as they grow.

## Mzizi: The Established Multi-Curriculum ERP

**Best for:** Mid-to-large schools, IGCSE schools, school groups, and institutions needing payroll and boarding management alongside academics.

Mzizi is one of the most established players in this comparison, operating since at least 2014 with 300+ schools and 100,000+ students across Kenya and Uganda. That track record matters: a system that has survived a decade of Kenyan school operations has been through term crunches, network outages, year-end migrations, and the 2019 CBC transition.

**Where Mzizi is strongest:**

**Multi-curriculum support.** Mzizi handles CBC, 8-4-4, and IGCSE within the same system. If your school runs multiple streams or is transitioning between curricula, this flexibility is genuinely useful and unusual.

**Operational depth.** HR and payroll processing, transport route optimization, boarding administration, and inventory management are all built in. For a large boarding school managing staff payroll, student transport routes, and hostel operations alongside academics, Mzizi covers the full stack.

**Fee reconciliation across payment methods.** The platform reconciles M-Pesa, bank transfers, and cash payments together — useful for schools that receive payments through multiple channels and need a unified view.

**Parent engagement.** The Mzizi Engage product offers 24/7 parent access to balances, attendance, and results via SMS and WhatsApp — not just email or a web portal.

**Where Mzizi has gaps:**

Pricing is not disclosed publicly — you need to request a quote. For a budget-conscious school, this makes it harder to evaluate Mzizi against other options without committing to a sales call.

Schools specifically focused on CBC primary education may find Mzizi's multi-curriculum breadth comes with more complexity than they need. The system is built to serve many school types; a dedicated CBC-only platform will feel more tailored to that specific workflow.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I switch systems later without losing my data?

Yes — from any reputable system. Before signing up with any vendor, ask: "Can I export all my data to CSV or Excel at any time?" and "What does the offboarding process look like?" Every system on this list stores your school's data — student records, fee history, academic results. That data belongs to you. A vendor who won't commit to a clean export process is a red flag regardless of how good their platform looks during the demo.

### Which system has the best M-Pesa integration?

All five platforms on this list support M-Pesa. The difference is in the depth of integration. Deeper integration means: (1) automatic payment matching to student accounts without manual entry, (2) real-time notifications when payment arrives, (3) auto-generated receipts, and (4) a unified dashboard showing M-Pesa, bank, and cash payments in one view. Ask any vendor to show you the M-Pesa flow in a live demo before committing — the difference between a real integration and a manual log-it-yourself process is significant.

### Is free school management software sustainable?

Free or freemium software can be sustainable if there's a clear business model behind it — paid tiers, enterprise contracts, or donor funding. The risk is that a free product's investment in support, security, and new features depends on that model remaining viable. Before committing your school's data long-term to a free platform, it's worth understanding how the company earns revenue. If they can't explain it, that's worth noting.

### Do any of these systems support NEMIS?

NEMIS (National Education Management Information System) integration is a regulatory requirement for Kenyan schools. DiraSchool stores all fields required for NEMIS reporting and provides CSV export — full NEMIS sync is on the roadmap. For other systems on this list, NEMIS support is not confirmed on their public websites. This is a specific question to ask during any demo: "Can I export student data in a NEMIS-compatible format, and do you support direct NEMIS sync?"

### How long does it take to get a school set up on a new system?

For most platforms on this list, expect 2–4 weeks from signup to fully live. The main time is spent on: importing existing student data (usually from your current register or Excel file), setting up your fee structures, and training your office staff and teachers. A vendor who provides hands-on onboarding support will dramatically reduce this time. A vendor who hands you a PDF manual and leaves you to it will take longer and create more frustration. Ask during your demo: "What does the onboarding process look like, and who supports us during setup?"
